Who We Are

A Small NGO with an
Enormous Mission

Safe Water for Every Child – Myanmar is a lean, agile non-government organisation dedicated to one goal: ensuring the Karen people of Myanmar's remote eastern mountains have access to clean, safe drinking water.

Founded in 2018 by environmental scientist Fleur Maidment and former Karen refugee Tha Blay, we work directly with trained local staff to install water filters, test water quality, deliver health education, and run community immunisation programs.

We are proudly partnered with Disaster Aid Australia and supported by WaterOAM and the Singapore Red Cross.

Community-Led

Local Karen staff lead all water testing, filter installation, and WASH education — sustainable change from within.

Radical Transparency

100% of donated funds go directly to programs on the ground. We publish our impact reports openly.

Rapid Response

Mobilised emergency relief after Myanmar's 2021 military coup and natural disaster events across the region.

Who We Serve

The Karen People of
Eastern Myanmar

The Karen are an indigenous people of subsistence farmers who have lived for centuries in the forested mountains along Myanmar's eastern border. They live in bamboo and thatch houses in communities that are often completely inaccessible — reachable only by plane, boat, or on foot.

Decades of military conflict have displaced hundreds of thousands of Karen people, forcing families into refugee camps with little access to clean water, sanitation, or healthcare. Waterborne disease — cholera, diarrhea, typhoid — remains a constant and deadly threat, especially for children.

Our filters have produced instant results, dramatically reducing diarrhea and cholera cases in every community we serve.

What We Do

Our Programs

We take a holistic approach to water security — clean water alone isn't enough. We pair every filter installation with health education, immunisation, and long-term community support.

Water Filter Installation

We deploy ROAMfilter Plus and SmartAid UltraFilter systems in schools, medical centres, and village hubs. Each unit produces up to 1,000 litres per hour and serves ~700 people for up to 10 years at less than $1 per person per year.

35,000+ people currently served

Community Water Testing

Our trained local teams test water sources across Karen State using portable field equipment, identifying contamination, advising on treatment, and monitoring quality over time to ensure lasting safety.

150+ communities tested

Immunisation Programs

We deliver essential vaccines to remote communities, protecting against malaria, cholera, dengue, and chikungunya — diseases that thrive where clean water is scarce and healthcare access is limited.

15,000+ shots delivered

WASH Education

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH) education empowers families to maintain safe water practices. We run interactive sessions in schools and community centres, distributing hygiene supplies including cups, soap, and safe water containers.

Every filter site covered

Mosquito Net Distribution

We distribute treated mosquito nets to families in endemic areas, providing critical protection against malaria and dengue — diseases that disproportionately affect young children in under-resourced communities.

Thousands of nets distributed

English Tutoring Program

Launched during COVID-19 on the Thai–Myanmar border, our tutoring program connects 150 volunteer tutors from around the world with Karen students, providing educational continuity and brighter futures beyond the camps.

150 volunteer tutors globally

Our Founder

Fleur Maidment —
From Conversation to Change

Fleur Maidment is an environmental scientist with a background in agriculture, forestry, and emergency planning. After relocating to Singapore in 2016, she became deeply interested in Myanmar's humanitarian challenges — and inspired by Peter Singer's book The Most Good You Can Do.

"The conversation that started it all was with a 24-year-old Karen refugee named Tha Blay. When we talked about what his community needed most, the answer was clear: clean water." — Fleur Maidment, Founder

Fleur first travelled to Myanmar with Tha Blay — now living in Nhill, Australia — and the two identified access to clean water as the most urgent, life-saving priority. Together they founded Safe Water for Every Child Myanmar in 2018.

Fleur holds a degree in agriculture and forestry from the University of Melbourne, where she was a resident of Queen's College. She describes her career as a journey into "a very male-dominated industry" before pivoting to humanitarian work that she finds deeply fulfilling.
